Our Courses

At DTU, we offer a wide variety of undergraduate and postgraduate courses designed to shape the leaders of tomorrow. Explore the courses available in each department below.

B.Tech Programs

Electronics & Communication Engineering (ECE)

This program covers the design, development, and application of electronic devices and communication systems.

Computer Engineering (COE)

Learn the fundamentals of computer hardware, software systems, and programming in this comprehensive course.

Mechanical Engineering (ME)

Focuses on mechanical systems, thermodynamics, materials science, and energy systems to prepare students for engineering challenges.

Electrical Engineering (EE)

Study electrical systems, power generation, transmission, and control systems for a variety of engineering applications.

Production & Industrial Engineering (PIE)

This course integrates industrial engineering and production processes, focusing on optimizing manufacturing and operations.

Civil Engineering (CE)

Civil Engineering at DTU focuses on the planning, design, and construction of infrastructure projects like roads, bridges, and buildings.

Environmental Engineering (ENE)

Gain expertise in sustainable solutions for environmental issues, including pollution control, water treatment, and waste management.

Chemical Engineering

Explore the principles of chemical processes, material synthesis, and product design to address challenges in industries like pharmaceuticals and energy.

Information Technology (IT)

This course covers the development and management of IT systems, focusing on software engineering, databases, and network security.

Bio-Technology (BT)

Explore the intersection of biology and technology, focusing on the application of biological systems for solving engineering problems.

Software Engineering (SE)

Develop software applications and gain knowledge of software development life cycles, design principles, and system integration.

Electrical and Electronics Engineering (EEE)

This course covers both electrical systems and electronics, focusing on energy systems, automation, and microelectronics.

Mechanical Engineering with Specialization in Automotive Engineering (MAM)

A specialized course focused on the design, development, and optimization of automotive systems and vehicle technologies.

Engineering Physics (EP)

Engineering Physics blends physics and engineering, preparing students for a deep understanding of applied physics and its role in engineering innovations.

Mathematics and Computing (MC)

This course provides a foundation in mathematics, computing, and algorithms, preparing students for careers in computational fields.
